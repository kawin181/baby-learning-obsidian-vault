# Newborn Sensory Development — NHS

## Key Points
- Vision is the least developed sense at birth; babies cannot see clearly beyond 30 cm
- Babies love hearing voices and watching faces; talk and sing during feeding
- Newborn hearing screening is done in hospital or within 4–5 weeks; can be done up to 3 months
- Rooting reflex: turns toward cheek touch, helps baby find food
- Sucking reflex: triggered when object placed in mouth
- Startle (Moro) reflex: extends arms/legs then recoils to sudden sound or movement
- Grasp reflex: closes fingers around objects placed in palm
- Most primitive reflexes disappear as baby develops — sucking reflex persists
- By 6 weeks: social smile appears, directed at primary caregiver
- By 3 months: holds small rattles momentarily, clasps hands together
- Head control increases to ~45 degrees on tummy by 3 months
- Brief weight-bearing on feet with support begins around 1–3 months
- Flat spots on head combined with head-turning preference may indicate plagiocephaly

## Raw Content

### Vision
At birth, sight is the least developed sense. Babies cannot focus clearly beyond ~30 cm. They can see light, gross shapes, and their caregiver's face. Eye contact with the caregiver's face is brief initially and includes natural looking-away periods.

### Hearing
Babies prefer the human voice from birth. Hearing screening is performed in hospital or within 4–5 weeks of birth. Parents are encouraged to talk and sing in exaggerated, sing-song tones to support early speech development.

### Reflexes (Primitive Sensory-Motor Responses)
- **Rooting reflex**: Baby turns toward the direction of cheek stimulation — helps locate food
- **Sucking reflex**: Triggered by oral contact with an object
- **Moro (Startle) reflex**: Response to sudden sound or movement — extends limbs then recoils
- **Palmar grasp reflex**: Closes hand around palm stimulation

### Motor Development (Linked to Touch/Proprioception)
- 0–4 weeks: Brief head lift in prone; equal arm/leg movement; reflexive grasp
- 1–3 months: 45-degree head lift in tummy time, brief foot weight-bearing, holds rattle
- By 3 months: Can briefly clasp hands

### Communication & Interaction
- 0–4 weeks: Brief face engagement
- ~6 weeks: Social smile at caregiver
- 1–3 months: Vocal experimentation (cooing)

## Warning Signs 🚨
- Flat spots on the head (rear or sides) combined with head-turning preference → may indicate plagiocephaly → refer to physiotherapy
- No social smile by 8 weeks
- Does not react to sounds or voices
