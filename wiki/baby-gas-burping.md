# Baby Gas & Burping Techniques

## Summary
Newborn gassiness is normal — the digestive system is still maturing. Effective burping during and after every feed, combined with good feeding posture and bottle selection, significantly reduces discomfort. Gas drops are optional and their clinical benefit is debated between AAP and Mayo Clinic.

## What Research Says
Burping works by allowing swallowed air to escape before it travels deeper into the digestive tract. The AAP and Mayo Clinic both recommend burping during and after each feeding session. Bottle-fed babies typically swallow more air than breastfed babies, especially if the nipple flow is too fast or the bottle angle allows air into the nipple.

**On gas drops (simethicone):** There is a notable split between major sources. The AAP notes that clinical studies show simethicone does not help with colic and increasingly advises against routine use. Mayo Clinic considers them generally safe at 20 mg up to 4×/day and notes "if they seem to help, there's no harm." Bottom line: not strongly recommended, but not harmful.

**Bicycle legs and tummy massage:** These mechanical techniques help move trapped intestinal gas along and are consistently endorsed across sources.

## Practical Guidelines

### Burping Positions (choose one that works for your baby)
1. **Over the shoulder** — baby upright, chin over your shoulder; support head; pat or rub back gently
2. **Seated on lap** — baby sitting upright on your thigh, one hand supporting chin and chest; pat back
3. **Face-down across lap** — baby prone across your knees, head higher than chest; pat back

### When to Burp
- Bottle-fed: every 2–3 oz during the feed, then again at the end
- Breastfed: every time you switch breasts, then at the end
- Anytime baby seems fussy, pulls off, or squirms during a feed

### Feeding Posture to Reduce Gas
- Hold baby at ~45° angle; ensure bottle nipple is always full of milk (not air)
- Choose slow-flow nipples for newborns
- For breastfed babies: ensure a good latch to minimize air ingestion
- Keep baby upright for 20–30 minutes after feeding

### Bottle Choice
- Curved bottles that collapse as they empty reduce air intake
- Vented or angled bottles also help
- Experiment — different babies respond to different bottles

### Gas Relief Exercises
- **Bicycle legs**: baby on back, gently pedal legs in cycling motion for 1–2 minutes
- **Tummy massage**: gentle clockwise circular strokes on abdomen
- **Tummy time** (supervised, when awake): gentle pressure on abdomen can help release gas

## Warning Signs 🚨
- Gas + vomiting, refusing feeds, or poor weight gain → see a doctor
- Blood in stool
- Inconsolable crying >3 hours/day (may indicate colic or other condition — see [[wiki/infant-colic]])
- Abdomen feels hard and distended
