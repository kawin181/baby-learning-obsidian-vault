# Newborn Skin Care & Baby Bath — AAP HealthyChildren

## Key Points
- WHO recommends delaying first bath 24 hours after birth (minimum 6 hours if culturally needed)
- Delaying 12 hours increases breastfeeding success by 166% in one study; preserves vernix (natural moisturizer)
- Sponge baths only until umbilical cord falls off (1–2 weeks)
- Frequency: 3 baths per week — more often dries out skin
- Water temperature: warm to wrist/elbow; max faucet temp 120°F to prevent scalds
- Wash face first with soap-free cloth; body top to bottom; diaper area last
- Products: mild, neutral-pH soap without additives; fragrance-free hypoallergenic lotion post-bath
- Cradle cap: harmless, self-resolving; loosen with soft-bristled brush
- Never leave baby unattended in bath, even for a second
- Avoid bath seats — drowning risk

## Raw Content
**First bath delay (WHO/AAP):**
- Wait 24 hours after birth (or ≥6 hours minimum)
- Vernix on skin = natural moisturizer, reduces infection risk; leave it on
- 12-hour delay → 166% increase in breastfeeding success (research cited)
- Helps maintain body temperature and blood sugar

**Sponge bath technique:**
- Baby on flat padded surface (not in water)
- Have all supplies within arm's reach before starting
- Wash face first with damp, soap-free cloth
- Wash body from top to bottom, keep rest wrapped in towel
- Attend to creases and skin folds
- Continue until umbilical cord stump falls off (1–2 weeks)

**Tub bath technique (once cord healed):**
- Fill with 2–3 inches of warm water
- Support head with one hand; guide in feet-first
- Wash quickly and efficiently; brief and gentle
- Never use bath seats (collapse/drowning risk)
- Never leave baby unattended

**Water safety:**
- Max faucet temp: 120°F (set hot water heater)
- Test water: wrist or elbow (not hand)
- Turn off faucet before placing baby

**Frequency:** 3 baths/week maximum. Over-bathing strips natural oils and dries skin.

**Products:**
- Mild, neutral-pH soap without dyes or fragrances (minimal use)
- Mild baby shampoo 2–3x/week
- Fragrance-free, hypoallergenic moisturizing lotion after bath while skin still slightly damp

**Skin conditions:**
- Cradle cap (scaly yellow patches on scalp): harmless, self-resolving; loosen with soft brush; anti-dandruff shampoo only if persistent (ask doctor)
- Dry skin: minimize soap; apply moisturizer; avoid bubble baths
- Baby acne: normal in first weeks; no treatment needed; gently wash with mild soap
