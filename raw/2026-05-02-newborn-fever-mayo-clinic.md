# Newborn Fever — Mayo Clinic Health System

## Key Points
- Fever in infants < 1 month: go to ER immediately, no waiting
- Fever in infants 1–3 months: see pediatrician same day; if not available, go to ER
- Any rectal temperature ≥ 100.4°F (38°C) is a fever requiring evaluation in this age group
- Fever may be the only sign of a serious bacterial infection in young infants
- Emergency warning signs beyond fever include: extreme sleepiness/hard to wake, floppy tone, inconsolable crying, signs of dehydration

## Raw Content
### Age-Specific Fever Guidelines
| Age | Action |
|-----|--------|
| Under 1 month | Emergency Room immediately |
| 1–3 months | Pediatrician same day; ER if pediatrician unavailable |
| Over 3 months | Call pediatrician; may monitor at home if otherwise well |

### Emergency Warning Signs (seek immediate care)
- Temperature ≥ 38°C (100.4°F) — at any age under 3 months
- Sleeping excessively or very difficult to awaken
- Floppy, limp muscle tone
- Crying more than usual, or crying that is inconsolable
- No wet diapers for 8–10 hours (dehydration)
- Crying without tears (dehydration)
- Dry mouth, sunken fontanelle (soft spot)
- Refusing to feed or drink fluids

### Why Fever Is Serious in Young Infants
- Immature immune system cannot mount localized defense
- Fever may be sole indicator of: meningitis, bacteremia, UTI, sepsis, pneumonia
- Cannot distinguish severity by appearance alone — professional evaluation required
- Serious bacterial infection rates vary by age: higher in youngest infants

### Fever Management at Home (only while awaiting care)
- Do NOT give acetaminophen (paracetamol) or ibuprofen to infants < 3 months without doctor instruction
- Sponge with lukewarm (not cold) water to help reduce temperature
- Keep baby lightly dressed
- Ensure breastfeeding continues if possible
- Do NOT use aspirin (risk of Reye's syndrome)
