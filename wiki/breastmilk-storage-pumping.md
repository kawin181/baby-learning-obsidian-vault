# Breastmilk Storage & Pumping

## Summary
Expressed breastmilk can be safely stored at room temperature for up to 4–6 hours, in the fridge for up to 4–8 days, and in the freezer for 6–12 months. The critical rules are to label everything with the date, never microwave breastmilk, never refreeze thawed milk, and store in small portions at the back of the fridge or freezer. Fathers can play a key role by giving one bottle of expressed milk during the night so the feeding parent gets a longer sleep stretch.

## What Research Says
- **Room temperature**: AAP = 4 hours (up to 6–8 if very clean expression); NHS = 6 hours. Both: chill as soon as possible.
- **Refrigerator (≤4°C)**: AAP = 4 days optimal; NHS = up to 8 days at back of fridge (3 days if temperature uncertain). Store at back, not door.
- **Freezer (≤−18°C)**: AAP = 9 months (standard) / 12 months (deep freeze); NHS = 6 months.
- **Cool bag with ice packs**: NHS = up to 24 hours (useful for commuting/travel).
- **Thawed milk**: AAP = use within 24 hours; NHS = use immediately. Both: never refreeze.
- **After baby drinks from bottle**: AAP = discard within 2 hours; NHS = discard within 1 hour. Both: never return to freezer.

## Practical Guidelines

### Expressing
1. **Wash hands** before pumping or hand-expressing.
2. **Choose your method**: Hand expression (free, useful in early days), manual pump (affordable), electric pump (fastest — available for hire in Thailand).
3. **Pump fit**: Ensure the flange/cup fits correctly — wrong size causes pain and nipple damage over time.
4. **Portion size**: Store in 60–120 ml (2–4 oz) portions to minimise waste.

### Storage Rules
1. **Label everything**: Date, time, volume, and baby's name (for childcare handover).
2. **Location**: Back of fridge or freezer — not the door (temperature fluctuates at door).
3. **Leave headroom**: Milk expands when frozen — do not fill container to the top.
4. **Container**: Sterilised glass, hard BPA-free plastic, or dedicated breast milk storage bags.

### Thawing & Warming
1. **Thaw overnight**: Place in fridge the night before — safest method.
2. **Quick thaw**: Jug of warm water or under running warm water.
3. **NEVER microwave**: Creates uneven hot spots that burn baby's mouth.
4. **Temperature test**: Drop on inside of wrist — should feel neutral (body temp).
5. **Never refreeze**: Once thawed or warmed, use or discard. Do not return to freezer.

### Father's Role
- Partner can give one expressed-milk bottle at night to allow the feeding parent a longer uninterrupted sleep block.
- This supports breastfeeding sustainability without reducing milk supply.
- Feeding a bottle does not require the feeding parent to pump at the same time unless supply maintenance is needed.

## Thai Context
In Thailand, electric pumps (Medela, Spectra, and local brands) are widely available in pharmacies and online marketplaces. Breast milk storage bags (ถุงเก็บน้ำนม) are sold at most major supermarkets. Note: standard Thai refrigerators often have the ice compartment inside the fridge compartment — milk stored there lasts up to 2 weeks (NHS guidance). A separate chest freezer gives the longest storage (up to 12 months).

## Warning Signs 🚨
- Expressed milk with unusual smell (slightly soapy is normal due to lipase; sour/rancid is not) — discard
- Thawed milk that has been sitting at room temperature for more than 2 hours — discard
- Milk heated in a microwave — discard (hot spots risk)
- Refrozen milk (milk that was previously thawed) — discard
- Pump parts with cracked or degraded membranes/valves — replace before use
