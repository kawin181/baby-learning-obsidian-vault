# Umbilical Cord Care — AAP HealthyChildren.org

## Key Points
- Dry cord care is the current standard in high-resource settings (US hospitals and developed countries)
- Do NOT apply alcohol or antiseptic agents to cord in hospital-born infants
- Keep stump dry and exposed to air to help dry the base
- Fold the front of the diaper down to avoid covering the stump
- Stump should fall off within 1–3 weeks (typically by 3 weeks)
- Sponge baths only until cord falls off — no tub baths
- Contact pediatrician if stump has not fallen off by 3 weeks
- For low-resource settings: WHO recommends 4% chlorhexidine application within 24 hours to reduce omphalitis and neonatal mortality

## Raw Content
The AAP recommends "dry cord care" for newborns born in hospitals in high-resource countries. This means:
- Keep the cord clean and dry
- Do not submerge the baby in water until the cord falls off
- Give sponge baths only
- Allow air to reach the stump
- Fold diaper below the stump line
- No need for antiseptics or alcohol in developed-country hospital births

Timeline: The cord normally dries and falls off within 1–3 weeks. If it is still attached beyond 3 weeks, consult a physician as it may signal an immune deficiency or other condition.

In low-resource community settings with high neonatal mortality (e.g., South Asia), WHO recommends applying 4% chlorhexidine solution or gel to the umbilical stump within 24 hours of birth, repeated daily for 3 days. This significantly reduces omphalitis and neonatal mortality.
