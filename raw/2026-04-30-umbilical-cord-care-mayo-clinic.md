# Umbilical Cord Care — Mayo Clinic

## Key Points
- Keep the stump dry; expose to air whenever possible
- Fold diaper below the stump or use newborn diapers with cut-out notch
- Sponge baths only until stump falls off (typically 7–21 days)
- Cord may pass through color changes: yellow-green → brown → black as it dries — this is normal
- Do NOT pull the stump off even if it seems ready; let it fall off naturally
- Warning signs of omphalitis (cord infection): redness spreading from base, swelling, pus, foul odor, fever, or baby acting ill

## Raw Content
Mayo Clinic's guidance on umbilical cord care:

**Normal cord changes:** The cord stump will change color from yellowish-green at birth to brown and then black as it dries. A small amount of dried blood at the base is normal.

**Keep it dry:**
- Expose to open air when possible
- Keep front of diaper folded below the stump
- Avoid covering the area with clothing that traps moisture

**Bathing:** Sponge-bathe only until the cord falls off. Do not immerse the baby in water.

**Do not pull:** Even when the cord looks almost detached, let it separate naturally.

**Omphalitis warning signs — seek medical care immediately if:**
- Redness extending more than 5 mm from the base of the stump
- Pus or yellowish discharge with a foul odor
- Swelling or warmth around the cord
- Baby has a fever (rectal temperature ≥ 38°C / 100.4°F)
- Baby seems unwell, lethargic, or not feeding
