# Infant Colic — Mayo Clinic

## Key Points
- Colic defined by "Rule of 3": crying ≥ 3 hours/day, ≥ 3 days/week, ≥ 3 weeks in an otherwise healthy infant
- Peaks around 6 weeks of age; resolves by 3–6 months
- Affects 10–40% of infants worldwide regardless of feeding method
- Cause is unknown; multiple hypotheses (immature gut, gut microbiome, feeding issues, hypersensitivity)
- Mainstay of treatment is parental reassurance — colic is benign and self-limited
- No medication proven effective; simethicone (gas drops) not shown to help
- Soothing techniques: rhythmic motion, white noise, pacifier, swaddling, warm bath
- Two-week trial of extensively hydrolyzed formula may help formula-fed infants
- Never shake a baby — parental frustration during colic episodes is a risk factor for shaken baby syndrome

## Raw Content
**Definition:** Colic is intense, unexplained crying in an otherwise healthy, well-fed baby. The classic Wessel criteria is: crying for more than 3 hours per day, more than 3 days per week, for more than 3 weeks.

**Prevalence:** 10–40% of infants globally; equal across breastfed and formula-fed babies.

**Peak and resolution:** Symptoms typically peak at 6 weeks and resolve by 3–4 months.

**Hypothesized causes:**
- Immature digestive system
- Imbalanced gut microbiome (low Lactobacillus)
- Overfeeding or underfeeding
- Food sensitivities (cow's milk protein)
- Childhood migraine equivalent
- Parental anxiety feedback loop

**Soothing strategies (Mayo Clinic):**
- Infant swing or rocking chair
- Car ride or stroller walk
- White noise (fan, vacuum, hairdryer)
- Pacifier
- Warm bath
- Gentle abdominal massage
- Swaddling
- Skin-to-skin contact

**What does NOT work:**
- Simethicone (Mylicon gas drops) — not proven effective
- Probiotics — insufficient evidence
- Gripe water — no scientific support
- Spinal manipulation or cranial osteopathy — no evidence, potentially harmful

**Safety warning:** Shaken Baby Syndrome risk increases during colic episodes. Parents must be counseled to put the baby down safely and walk away if feeling overwhelmed.
