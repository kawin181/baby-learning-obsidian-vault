# Baby Nail Care

## Summary
Newborn nails grow about 0.1 mm per day and need trimming roughly once a week to prevent self-scratching. The safest method is to trim with baby nail clippers or a fine emery board while the infant is asleep. AAP advises that mittens are rarely necessary — proper regular nail trimming eliminates the need for them.

## What Research Says

### Growth and Frequency
Infant fingernails grow approximately 0.1 mm per day — faster in warmer months and in younger infants. Most newborns need trimming weekly. Some babies are born with nails already long enough to scratch (AAP, NHS).

### Tools
Three options, all acceptable:
1. **Baby nail clippers** — most precise for clean cuts
2. **Small round-ended safety scissors** — blunt tip reduces risk of poking the finger
3. **Fine emery board / nail file** — zero sharp-edge risk, ideal for the first week or two when nails are very soft

**Avoid:** adult-sized clippers or scissors (too large, poor control), and biting or peeling nails (introduces oral bacteria, risk of paronychia — nail bed infection).

### Technique
1. Wait until baby is in deep sleep (hands relaxed, less likely to flinch)
2. Hold the hand firmly and gently
3. Press the fingertip pad downward to separate skin from nail
4. Clip or trim following the nail's natural curve
5. Smooth sharp edges with an emery board
6. **Toenails:** trim straight across — not curved — to prevent ingrown nails

### Mittens: Rarely Needed
AAP explicitly states mittens are "rarely needed for newborns." Regular nail trimming prevents the scratching that mittens are meant to address. If mittens are used, limit to sleep time — babies need unrestricted hand exploration while awake for sensory and motor development. Bluish or cool hands and feet are normal in newborns and are not a reason to add mittens.

## Practical Guidelines
1. **Check nails weekly** — they grow fast; don't wait until you see scratches
2. **Trim during deep sleep** — deepest sleep gives the longest still window
3. **Start with emery board if nervous** — zero cut risk; graduall move to clippers
4. **One finger at a time** — never rush
5. **Toenails straight, fingernails curved** — follow natural shape for each
6. **Skip mittens** — baby's hands need to be free during wake time for development
7. **Accidental cut?** Apply gentle pressure with a clean cloth for a few minutes — it will stop; do not use adhesive bandages (choking risk)

## Thai Context
Some Thai families use mittens full-time "to keep hands clean" or warm. This is not recommended beyond necessary — constant mittens prevent the baby from feeling different textures, which is important for early sensory development. Keep mittens for sleep only if needed, and prioritize regular nail trimming instead.

## Warning Signs 🚨
- Redness, swelling, warmth, or pus around the nail bed → paronychia (nail bed infection) → see pediatrician, may need antibiotics
- Bleeding after accidental cut that does not stop in 5 minutes
- Nail appears dark or detaching without clear cause
- Ingrown toenail causing persistent redness or swelling in older baby
