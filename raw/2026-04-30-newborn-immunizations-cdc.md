# Newborn Immunizations — CDC

## Key Points
- CDC 2025 schedule: HepB within 24 hours of birth is top priority
- RSV immunization (nirsevimab): within 1 week of birth during Oct–Mar, ideally in birth hospitalization
- At 1–2 months: DTaP is best protection from diphtheria, tetanus, whooping cough
- Vaccination protects against 16+ potentially harmful diseases
- Schedule updated July 2025; fully consistent with AAP schedule

## Raw Content
### Birth Vaccines (within 24 hours)
- **Hepatitis B (HepB)**: given within 24 hours of birth regardless of maternal HepB status
  - If mother is HepB positive: baby also receives Hepatitis B Immune Globulin (HBIG) at birth
  - Critical: HepB can be passed from mother to baby during delivery; early vaccination prevents chronic infection

### RSV Immunization at Birth (seasonal)
- **Nirsevimab (Beyfortus)**: monoclonal antibody, not a traditional vaccine — provides immediate passive immunity
- Recommended for infants born Oct–March, within 1 week of birth (ideally before leaving hospital)
- Also recommended for all infants <8 months entering their first RSV season
- RSV is leading cause of hospitalization in infants <12 months in the US

### 2-Month Visit Vaccines
- **DTaP (dose 1)**: Diphtheria, Tetanus, Pertussis
  - Pertussis (whooping cough) is most dangerous for babies <3 months; can be fatal
  - Mother's Tdap booster during pregnancy provides some early protection
- **IPV (dose 1)**: Polio vaccine
- **Hib (dose 1)**: Haemophilus influenzae b — prevents bacterial meningitis
- **PCV15 or PCV20 (dose 1)**: Pneumococcal — prevents pneumonia and meningitis
- **Rotavirus (dose 1)**: Oral drops — prevents severe gastroenteritis

### Safety
- All vaccines on schedule are extensively tested for safety and efficacy
- Combination vaccines (PEDIARIX, Pentacel) reduce number of injections
- Post-vaccination reactions: soreness at injection site, low-grade fever, fussiness — normal and temporary
- Severe allergic reactions (anaphylaxis) are rare (<1 per million doses)

### Thai Context Note
- Thailand's EPI (Expanded Programme on Immunization) includes BCG and HepB at birth
- BCG (tuberculosis) is given at birth in Thailand — not on US schedule
- DTaP and other 2-month vaccines align with Thai national schedule
