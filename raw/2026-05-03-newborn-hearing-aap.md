# Newborn Hearing Development & Screening — AAP (HealthyChildren.org)

## Key Points
- ~6,000 US newborns/year are born with some degree of hearing impairment
- AAP recommends screening before 1 month of age, ideally before hospital discharge (1–2 days after birth)
- Tests: OAE (otoacoustic emissions) and AABR (automated auditory brainstem response)
- If screen not passed: full diagnostic hearing test by 3 months of age
- Early intervention by 6 months = best language outcomes
- Hearing milestones 0–3 months: startle at loud noise; calm to parent's voice; beginning to turn toward sounds by 3–4 months
- Additional screenings recommended at ages 4, 5, 6, 8, 10 and through age 21

## Raw Content

### Why Screen
Approximately 6,000 US newborns are born annually with atypical hearing. Without early detection, consequences include delayed speech and language development, learning difficulties, and social challenges. Intervention before 6 months of age produces the best language development outcomes.

### Timing (AAP EHDI 1-3-6 Guidelines)
- **Screen by 1 month** — ideally within 1–2 days of birth before hospital discharge
- **Diagnose by 3 months** — if failed screen, complete audiological evaluation before 3 months
- **Intervene by 6 months** — hearing aids, cochlear implant evaluation, or speech-language services

### Test Methods
**OAE (Otoacoustic Emissions):**
- Tiny probe placed in ear canal
- Soft sounds played; inner ear hair cells produce faint echo sounds (OAE) back
- Normal hair cells generate OAE; damaged hair cells do not
- Quick, non-invasive; infant may be awake

**AABR (Automated Auditory Brainstem Response):**
- Soft earphones and 3 sensors on head/neck
- Measures how auditory nerve and brainstem respond to sound
- Infant must be asleep or very still
- More comprehensive than OAE; used as follow-up or when OAE unclear

### Risk Factors for Hearing Loss
- NICU hospitalization (especially >48 hours)
- Family history of childhood hearing loss
- Congenital infections (CMV, rubella, syphilis, herpes)
- Head/face abnormalities (cleft palate, ear malformation)
- Hyperbilirubinemia requiring exchange transfusion
- Ototoxic medications during pregnancy

### Developmental Milestones — Hearing (0–3 months)
- **Newborn:** Startles at loud sudden sounds (Moro reflex)
- **0–2 months:** Calms or stirs when parent speaks; recognizes mother's voice
- **3 months:** Begins to turn head or eyes toward sound source
- **Note:** A startle response does NOT confirm adequate hearing for speech/language learning — formal screening is required
