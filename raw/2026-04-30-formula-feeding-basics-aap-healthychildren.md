# Formula Feeding Basics — AAP HealthyChildren.org

## Key Points
- AAP recommends iron-fortified formula for infants who are not breastfed, from birth to 12 months
- Newborns: 1–2 oz (30–60 ml) per feed in the first week
- By end of first month: 3–4 oz (90–120 ml) per feed, ~32 oz total per day
- General rule: ~2.5 oz of formula per pound of body weight per day
- Feeding frequency: every 3–4 hours for formula-fed babies
- Between 2–4 months (or >12 lbs), most babies can drop nighttime feeds
- Never exceed 32 oz (960 ml) per day
- Watch for hunger cues (lip-smacking) and fullness cues (fidgeting, turning away)
- NEVER dilute formula with extra water — risk of electrolyte imbalance and seizures
- Formula does not need to be warmed; if warming, NEVER use microwave

## Raw Content
The AAP recommends iron-fortified infant formula for all infants not receiving breast milk. Formula provides complete nutrition for the first 12 months of life.

**Feeding amounts by age:**
- First week: 1–2 oz per feeding (every 2–3 hours)
- 1 month: 3–4 oz per feeding (every 3–4 hours)
- 2–4 months: 4–5 oz per feeding
- Maximum daily intake: 32 oz (960 ml)

**Hunger and fullness cues:**
- Hungry: lip-smacking, rooting, sucking on hands
- Full: fidgeting, distractibility, turning head away from bottle

**Formula preparation safety:**
- Use exact measurements — too much water reduces nutrients and can cause hyponatremia (low sodium) and seizures
- Use water from a safe source; boil tap water if safety is unknown
- Powdered formula: mix with hot water (~70°C/158°F) to kill Cronobacter bacteria, then cool before feeding
- Never microwave formula — creates hot spots that can burn baby's mouth

**Storage:**
- Prepared formula can be stored in the refrigerator for up to 24 hours
- Discard any formula left in bottle after a feeding (saliva + formula = bacteria growth)
- Use prepared formula within 1 hour of feeding start or within 2 hours of preparation if not refrigerated
