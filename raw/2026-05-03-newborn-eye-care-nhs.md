# Newborn Eye Care & Blocked Tear Ducts — NHS (Hull University Teaching Hospitals)

## Key Points
- Watery/sticky eyes from blocked tear duct are common and almost always resolve on their own
- 9 out of 10 cases resolve within the first year; problem may still resolve independently by age 5
- NHS massage technique: small finger, downward pressure from inner eyelid corner toward nose bridge, max 10 strokes, twice daily
- Hygiene care: wipe crusting with cooled boiled water and cotton wool
- Signs of infection → see GP (antibiotic drops)
- Surgery (probing) considered only if blocked tear duct persists past 12 months or repeated infections occur

## Raw Content

### Symptoms
- Tears pooling at corners of eye or spilling down cheeks
- Sticky, crusty eyes upon waking (discharge accumulates overnight)
- Can worsen in cold weather or during respiratory infections (nasal congestion)
- Red, inflamed eye (conjunctivitis) may develop as a complication

### Prognosis
- 9 in 10 cases resolve spontaneously within first year of life
- Even if unresolved at 1 year, may still open independently by age 5 without intervention
- Surgery is only considered when the duct remains blocked past 12 months with recurrent infections

### Massage Technique (Step-by-Step)
1. Wash hands thoroughly and dry them
2. Using the small (little) finger, locate the inner corner of the eyelid (near the nose bridge)
3. Apply gentle downward pressure, sliding the finger toward the mouth
4. Perform up to 10 strokes per session
5. Do this twice daily
6. Do NOT push sideways or upward; motion is strictly downward

### Hygiene Care
- Clean crusting gently with cooled boiled water and a clean piece of cotton wool
- Use a fresh piece of cotton wool for each wipe
- Wipe from inner to outer corner of the eye
- Moisturise skin below eye if it becomes sore from constant tear spillage

### When to See a Doctor
- Red eye (conjunctivitis) develops — needs antibiotic drops
- Blocked duct persists past 12 months of age
- Repeated eye infections occur despite massage and hygiene care

### Surgical Option (Probing)
- Done as a day procedure under brief general anaesthesia
- A thin probe is passed through the tear duct to open the blockage
- Post-op complications: mild nosebleed (common), rarely persistent symptoms
