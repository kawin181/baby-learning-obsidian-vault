# Newborn Sleep Patterns & Schedules — AAP / HealthyChildren.org

## Key Points
- Newborns sleep approximately 16–17 hours per day in 1–2 hour bursts
- Regular sleep cycles do not develop until approximately 4 months of age
- Babies cannot distinguish day from night at birth — circadian rhythm develops gradually
- Keep nighttime interactions quiet, calm, and low-light to help babies learn night vs day
- Increasing daytime wakefulness helps extend nighttime sleep stretches
- Room-sharing (not bed-sharing) for at least the first 6 months reduces SIDS risk
- Always place baby on back to sleep (on a firm, flat surface)

## Raw Content

### Sleep Duration
Newborns sleep about 16 to 17 hours per day, but individual needs vary. Sleep occurs in short bursts — typically 1 to 2 hours at a time. Total daily sleep decreases as the baby matures.

### Sleep Cycle Development
Babies do not have regular sleep cycles until approximately 4 months of age. Before this, sleep is fragmented and unpredictable. Infant sleep cycles are approximately 45–50 minutes (much shorter than the adult 90-minute cycle), which explains frequent night waking.

### Day/Night Confusion
Newborns have no circadian rhythm at birth. The internal body clock (circadian rhythm) is entrained gradually by light exposure, feeding schedules, and social cues. Practical strategies:
- Daytime: Keep lights bright, maintain normal household noise, engage with baby when awake
- Night: Keep lights dim, speak softly, minimise stimulation during feeds and nappy changes

### Helping Baby Sleep
- Keep nighttime interactions calm and minimal
- Do not immediately rush to soothe every sound — allow brief self-settling attempts
- Avoid creating sleep associations that require parent intervention to maintain (rocking to sleep every time)
- Note: placing baby "drowsy but awake" is a technique recommended from 4 months; in the newborn phase, feeding to sleep is expected

### Safe Sleep Environment
- Room-share for at least the first 6 months
- Flat, firm, non-inclined surface
- Back to sleep always
- No soft bedding, pillows, or bumpers

### Sleep Milestones
- 0–4 weeks: 16–17 hours/day; no predictable schedule; wakes every 2–3 hours for feeds
- 1–2 months: slight increase in sleep consolidation; longest stretch may reach 3–4 hours at night
- 3 months: beginning of circadian rhythm entrainment; some babies begin giving one longer 4–5 hour nighttime stretch
