# Newborn Vision Development — NHS

## Key Points
- Newborns can see but vision is not focused; develops gradually over first few months
- Eye squint/rolling is normal in newborns — should resolve by 4 months
- Newborn physical examination (within first 72 hours) includes eye check
- Parents should see GP/health visitor if eyes remain misaligned beyond 4 months
- Babies develop from what they see, hear, and experience from day one

## Raw Content

### What Newborns Can See
- "New babies can see, but their vision is not very focused"
- Vision develops gradually over first few months
- Not specific but implies significant visual progression in this period

### Newborn Eye Check
- All babies offered newborn physical examination within first 72 hours of birth
- Eyes included in standard check: looking for structural problems and gross visual deficits
- Parents should flag any eye concerns to health visitor or GP who can refer for formal eye test

### Eye Development Timeline
- Birth: vision unfocused, basic light and shape perception
- Newborn period: eyes may occasionally roll or squint — normal
- By 4 months: squinting/misalignment should resolve
- If persistent misalignment after 4 months → see GP (may indicate strabismus)

### Stimulation (General NHS Development Guidance)
- Babies develop from everything they see, hear, and experience from birth
- Face-to-face interaction from day one is important
- All sensory input contributes to brain development including visual stimulation

### When to Seek Help
- Eyes still misaligned or squinting after 4 months
- Any concern about baby's ability to see
- Pupil looks white rather than black (immediate referral)
- Baby not making eye contact by 3 months
