# Cradle Cap (Seborrheic Dermatitis) — NHS (UK)

## Key Points
- Harmless skin condition, very common in babies
- Not itchy, not painful, does not bother baby
- Cause unknown, not contagious
- **DO NOT use olive oil or peanut oil** — damages the skin barrier
- Safe: coconut oil, emollient moisturizers
- See GP if it spreads to whole body, bleeds, leaks fluid, or won't resolve

## Raw Content

### What It Is
A harmless skin condition common in babies. Presents as patches of greasy, scaly skin on the scalp and face. Usually self-resolving.

### Appearance
- White or yellow crusty scales that flake
- Dry skin flakes (if diaper area affected)
- Skin underneath may appear pink or red (or lighter/darker tone depending on skin color)
- NOT itchy or painful

### Affected Areas
- Scalp (primary)
- Face
- Skin creases
- Diaper area

### Treatment (NHS)

**Recommended:**
- Apply emollient moisturizer or coconut oil to soften/loosen scales
- Gently brush scalp with soft brush, then wash with mild baby shampoo
- Bathe regularly with water + emollient instead of soap
- Apply barrier cream to diaper area if affected

**AVOID:**
- **Olive oil** — damages the skin barrier (NHS current guidance)
- **Peanut oil** — allergy risk
- Adult shampoos or strongly fragranced products
- Picking or scratching crusts (risk of infection)

### When to See a GP
- Cradle cap persists after weeks of home treatment
- Spreads to cover entire body
- Signs of infection: bleeding, fluid leaking, swelling

### Duration
Typically resolves within several months; self-limiting in almost all cases
