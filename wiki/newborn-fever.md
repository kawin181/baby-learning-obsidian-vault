# Newborn Fever

## Summary
Any rectal temperature of 38°C (100.4°F) or higher in an infant under 3 months is a medical emergency requiring same-day evaluation — no exceptions. Young infants have immature immune systems that cannot fight off serious infections like meningitis, sepsis, or UTI without professional help. The rule is simple: fever + under 3 months = call the doctor immediately.

## What Research Says
- **AAP 2021 Guideline**: Fever in infants 8–60 days old requires structured evaluation. Three age sub-groups (8–21 days, 22–28 days, 29–60 days) have different risk profiles and workup requirements.
- **Incidence**: ~14 per 1,000 healthy full-term infants develop fever at 8–60 days old.
- **Infections found in febrile young infants**: UTI (>10%), bacteremia, pneumonia, sepsis, meningitis (<0.05% but life-threatening).
- **Biomarker-based risk stratification**: AAP guidelines use procalcitonin and absolute neutrophil count (ANC) to classify low vs. high risk, potentially allowing some 29–60 day infants to avoid lumbar puncture.
- **Temperature measurement**: Rectal thermometry is gold standard; ear thermometers unreliable under 6 months; forehead strips not accurate.

## Practical Guidelines
1. **Under 1 month with fever** → Go to Emergency Room immediately. Do not wait for a callback.
2. **1–3 months with fever** → Call pediatrician for same-day appointment. If unavailable, go to ER.
3. **Take temperature correctly**: Use a digital rectal thermometer with petroleum jelly; insert 0.5–1 inch; hold still until beep.
4. **While traveling to care**: Dress baby lightly, sponge with lukewarm water to keep comfortable.
5. **Do NOT give medication**: No acetaminophen, ibuprofen, or aspirin to infants under 3 months without explicit doctor instruction.
6. **Continue breastfeeding**: Breast milk provides hydration and immune support during transit to care.

## Thai Context
- Thai hospitals (Sikarin, Siriraj) follow similar thresholds: any fever in a newborn warrants urgent evaluation.
- Traditional practice of wrapping baby tightly ("ห่อตัวแน่น") can cause overheating — avoid.
- In Thailand, sponging with warm water (เช็ดตัวด้วยน้ำอุ่น) is recommended as comfort measure while seeking care.
- Do not apply traditional herbal compresses or home remedies to reduce fever in newborns.

## Warning Signs 🚨
- Rectal temperature ≥ 38°C (100.4°F) in any infant under 3 months → **immediate care**
- Under 1 month with any fever → **ER immediately**
- Extreme sleepiness, very hard to wake
- Floppy or limp muscle tone
- Inconsolable crying
- No wet diapers for 8–10 hours
- No tears when crying
- Dry mouth, sunken fontanelle (soft spot on head)
- Refusing to feed
- Rapid or labored breathing
- Bluish color to lips or fingernails → **call emergency services**
