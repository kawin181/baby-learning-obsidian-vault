# Pacifier Use & Safety in Newborns

## Summary
Pacifiers (dummies) are safe and beneficial for newborns when used correctly — offering one at every nap and bedtime is one of the most evidence-backed ways to reduce SIDS risk. If breastfeeding, wait 3–4 weeks for nursing to be established before introducing one. Choose a one-piece moulded pacifier with a shield at least 1.5 inches across, and never attach it to a cord, crib, or clothing.

## What Research Says
- **SIDS risk reduction**: The AAP strongly endorses pacifier use at nap and bedtime as protective against SIDS; the NHS is more cautious but still recommends considering dummy use at sleep time. The protective effect persists even after the pacifier falls out — reinsertion is not required.
- **Breastfeeding**: Both AAP and NHS agree — delay introduction until breastfeeding is well established (~3–4 weeks). Once nursing is established, pacifier use does not negatively impact breastfeeding.
- **Dental & speech**: Extended use beyond 2–4 years may cause dental misalignment. NHS research links prolonged dummy use to reduced babbling and potential speech sound delays. Restricting use to sleep times limits this risk.
- **Formula-fed babies**: Can start pacifier use from birth with no restrictions.

## Practical Guidelines
1. **Timing**: Wait 3–4 weeks if breastfeeding; from birth if formula-feeding.
2. **Consistency**: Offer at every nap and bedtime — consistent use confers the most protection.
3. **Choosing a safe pacifier**: One-piece moulded plastic; shield ≥1.5 inches (3.8 cm) with ventilation holes; no detachable parts.
4. **Cleaning**: Sterilise before first use (boil or dishwasher). Clean frequently for the first 6 months. Do not "clean" by sucking the pacifier yourself.
5. **Never attach**: Do not tie to crib, neck, hand, clothing clip, or stuffed toy — strangulation and choking risk.
6. **Feeding priority**: Only offer when certain baby is not hungry. Never use to delay or replace feeds.
7. **Inspect regularly**: Replace if rubber/silicone is discoloured, torn, or sticky.
8. **Reduce gradually**: Once baby is babbling and communicating, remove dummy during awake time. Aim to wean by 6–12 months.

## Thai Context
No specific Thai Ministry of Public Health (กรมอนามัย) guidelines found on pacifier use. Thai hospital practice generally follows WHO/AAP guidance. It is common in Thai culture to offer dummies early; reassure fathers to wait until breastfeeding is established before introducing.

## Warning Signs 🚨
- Pacifier attached to any cord, ribbon, or string around baby's neck
- Using pacifier to persistently delay feedings in a hungry baby
- Pacifier with cracked, sticky, or discoloured nipple still in use
- Pacifier used during all waking hours beyond 3–6 months (limits babbling development)
- Any sign the pacifier nipple is detaching or becoming loose
