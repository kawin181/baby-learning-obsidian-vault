# Cradle Cap (Seborrheic Dermatitis) — AAP HealthyChildren.org

## Key Points
- Extremely common: affects ~70% of 3-month-old babies
- Appears between 3 weeks and 2 months after birth
- Not itchy, not contagious, not caused by poor hygiene
- Cause unknown: possibly yeast reaction or maternal hormone-triggered oil gland overproduction
- Resolves on its own within weeks to months (usually by 1 year)
- Safe treatments: mineral oil, coconut oil, petroleum jelly + gentle baby shampoo
- See doctor if scales become crusted, weepy, or have pus

## Raw Content

### What It Is
A type of seborrheic dermatitis found on the head. Oily, scaly rash on the scalp that is harmless and self-limiting.

### When It Appears
Typically develops between 3 weeks and 2 months after birth. Usually resolves by age 1.

### Symptoms
- Thick, yellowish, oily scales on scalp
- Scales or redness on face, neck folds, behind ears
- Can also appear in armpits, diaper area
- Not itchy or uncomfortable — does not bother baby
- Not contagious

### Cause
Unknown. Two theories:
1. Reaction to common skin yeast (Malassezia)
2. Maternal hormones causing baby's oil (sebaceous) glands to overproduce
Not caused by allergies, infection, or poor hygiene

### Treatment Steps (AAP)
1. Wash hair every other day with mild baby shampoo
2. During shampooing: gently loosen scales with soft brush or comb
3. For stubborn/thick scales: apply mineral oil, coconut oil, or petroleum jelly and leave overnight before washing
4. Do NOT scratch or pick scales — risk of infection

### When to See a Doctor
- Scales become crusted, weepy, or produce pus
- Foul odor
- Apparent discomfort or irritation
- Bleeding
- Swelling

### Duration
Usually resolves within weeks to months; most cases clear by 12 months
