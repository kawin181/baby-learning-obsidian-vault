# Kangaroo Care & Skin-to-Skin Contact — AAP HealthyChildren.org

## Key Points
- Kangaroo care originated in South America as a method to keep premature infants warm
- Involves holding a diaper-clad infant directly against a caregiver's bare chest
- Both parents (mother AND father) can provide kangaroo care
- Benefits are apparent even with brief daily sessions
- Recommended to start as soon as possible after birth
- Can and should continue after hospital discharge (e.g., mornings or after bathing)

## Raw Content

### Benefits for Infants
- Provides warmth and stabilizes heartbeat and breathing
- Increases time in deep sleep and quiet alert states
- Reduces crying
- Promotes weight gain
- Enhances breastfeeding outcomes
- Facilitates bonding and releases feel-good hormones (oxytocin)

### Who Can Participate
- Both parents can provide kangaroo care
- Hospitals typically offer comfortable rocking chairs and privacy screens
- Caregivers should wear front-opening clothing
- Baby positioned upright or with head against chest

### Timing & Continuation
- Start as soon as possible after birth — especially important for premature infants
- Continue skin-to-skin after hospital discharge
- Good times at home: mornings or after bathing
- Developed originally for premature infants but benefits apply to all newborns

### Evidence Base
- Even brief daily sessions produce measurable benefits
- Particularly well-studied in premature infants
- AAP actively promotes immediate KMC (iKMC) globally in collaboration with WHO
