# Baby Crying: Understanding and Soothing — Mayo Clinic

## Key Points
- Dr. Harvey Karp's 5 S's are evidence-based soothing techniques proven to calm newborns
- The 5 S's: Swaddle, Side/stomach position (when held), Shush, Swing, Suck
- Swaddling triggers the calming reflex by mimicking the womb environment
- Shushing (loud white noise) mimics sounds the baby heard in utero
- Sucking activates the baby's calming reflex independent of hunger
- Gentle rhythmic swinging mimics fetal movement experienced in the womb
- Parents should try multiple techniques — no single method works for all babies
- Parental stress and shaking risk: NEVER shake a baby; put baby down safely and seek support

## Raw Content
Mayo Clinic outlines understanding infant crying as a key skill for new parents. The most important framework for soothing a newborn is the "5 S's" method developed by pediatrician Dr. Harvey Karp.

**The 5 S's (evidence-based soothing sequence):**

1. **Swaddle** (ห่อตัว): Wrap baby snugly in a blanket with arms at sides. Mimics the secure feeling of the womb. Reduces startling (Moro reflex). Stop swaddling once baby can roll (typically 2 months).

2. **Side/Stomach position** (จับนอนตะแคงหรือท่าคว่ำ — when held, NOT for unsupervised sleep): Hold baby on side or stomach when comforting. This position is calming; always return to back for sleep.

3. **Shush** (เสียงดัง): Use loud, rhythmic "shhhh" sound near baby's ear, or white noise (vacuum, fan, app). Must be as loud as the baby's crying to be effective initially.

4. **Swing** (ไกวเปล): Small, rapid, jiggling movements while supporting the head. Car rides, rocking chairs, or gentle bouncing all activate this reflex. Movement should be smooth, never jerky.

5. **Suck** (ดูด): Offering a pacifier, a clean finger, or allowing sucking at the breast. Sucking activates the parasympathetic nervous system and is deeply calming even without milk.

**Effectiveness:**
- Using all 5 S's together is more effective than using individual techniques
- Most effective during the "fourth trimester" (0–3 months)
- Calming reflex diminishes after 3–4 months as the nervous system matures

**Caregiver safety:**
- Parental exhaustion combined with prolonged crying creates significant risk for shaken baby syndrome
- Resources: if overwhelmed, call trusted family member or contact local support line
- Brief periods of safe, supervised crying will not harm the baby
