# Baby Growth Spurts & Cluster Feeding — NHS

## Key Points
- Cluster feeding = baby wanting to feed constantly or very frequently over a period of time
- Most common in first 3–4 months of life
- Is completely normal — not a sign of low milk supply or that baby is unwell
- Baby can seem fussier, unsettled, and harder to settle during cluster feeding periods
- May feel like baby is "constantly attached" — this is normal demand signalling
- Supply catches up with demand in a few days — do not supplement unless advised by midwife or doctor
- Watch for fullness cues: fingers spreading, dribbling milk, stopping sucking, turning head away

## Raw Content

### Definition
Cluster feeding = wanting to feed even more frequently (sometimes constantly) over a period of time. Often occurs in bursts lasting hours or days.

### When It Happens
- First 3–4 months is most common window
- Often tied to growth spurts at approximately: 7–10 days, 3 weeks, 6 weeks, 3 months
- Can happen during day or night (often early evening = "evening cluster feeding")
- Does not mean you don't have enough milk

### What to Expect
- Baby feeds every 30–60 minutes during cluster periods
- Baby seems grumpy, unsettled, hard to put down
- Parents feel exhausted and may doubt their supply
- Duration: typically 2–7 days before settling

### For Breastfeeding Parents
- Keep nursing on demand — this is how supply is established
- Don't supplement formula unless midwife/doctor recommends
- Ensure good latch to prevent nipple damage during high-frequency nursing
- Take care of yourself: rest between feeds, eat and drink well

### For Bottle-Feeding Parents
- Watch fullness cues carefully to avoid overfeeding
- Signs of fullness: spreading fingers/toes, milk dribbling from mouth, stopping sucking, head turning away, refusing bottle
- Don't force baby to finish a bottle
- Use paced bottle feeding technique

### Reassurance
- "Very normal and nothing to be concerned about"
- Milk supply responds to demand and typically increases within 2–4 days
- Things will settle down
