# Newborn Immunizations

## Summary
The 0–3 month period is when infants are most vulnerable to life-threatening infections. The AAP/CDC 2025–2026 schedule begins with Hepatitis B at birth and adds 5–6 vaccines at the 2-month visit. Thailand's national EPI schedule broadly aligns but adds BCG at birth. All recommended vaccines have an excellent safety record supported by decades of data.

## What Research Says
The vaccine schedule is designed to provide immunity at the earliest possible moment — before maternal antibodies wane (typically by 2 months). Multiple vaccines given simultaneously at 2 months do not overwhelm the infant immune system; a healthy baby can respond to thousands of antigens daily. Delaying or spreading out vaccines extends the window of vulnerability without reducing adverse event risk.

**RSV immunization (nirsevimab/Beyfortus)** is a new addition to the 2025 schedule — a monoclonal antibody (not a traditional vaccine) that provides immediate passive immunity. Given within 1 week of birth during RSV season (Oct–Mar), it reduces RSV hospitalizations by ~75%.

**On the autism question:** The proposed link between vaccines and autism has been studied extensively in millions of children. No credible scientific evidence supports a connection. The original 1998 Wakefield paper was retracted due to fraud and ethical violations.

## Practical Guidelines

### Recommended Schedule (0–3 Months) — US/AAP/CDC 2025

| Age | Vaccine | Disease Protected Against |
|-----|---------|--------------------------|
| Birth (within 24h) | HepB dose 1 | Hepatitis B (liver disease/cancer) |
| 1–2 months | HepB dose 2 | Hepatitis B |
| 2 months | DTaP dose 1 | Diphtheria, Tetanus, Pertussis (whooping cough) |
| 2 months | IPV dose 1 | Polio |
| 2 months | Hib dose 1 | Bacterial meningitis (H. influenzae b) |
| 2 months | PCV15/20 dose 1 | Pneumonia, meningitis (pneumococcal) |
| 2 months | Rotavirus dose 1 | Severe diarrhea/dehydration |
| Birth–8 mo (Oct–Mar) | Nirsevimab | RSV (respiratory syncytial virus) |

### Thai National EPI Schedule (additions/differences)
- **BCG** (Bacillus Calmette-Guérin): given at birth in Thailand — protects against severe TB (tuberculosis); NOT on US schedule
- **HepB**: given at birth in Thailand — same as US schedule
- **DTP + HepB + Hib combo**: at 2, 4, 6 months — aligns with US DTaP/Hib/HepB schedule
- Parents in Thailand should follow the schedule at their local health clinic (สาธารณสุข)

### What to Expect at the 2-Month Visit
- Baby will typically receive 3–4 injections plus 1 oral dose (Rotavirus)
- Normal reactions for 24–48 hours: soreness/redness at injection sites, mild fever, fussiness, sleepiness
- Comfort measures: feed immediately after shots (nursing/bottle soothes); hold baby; acetaminophen (Tylenol) for fever if needed — do NOT give aspirin or ibuprofen to infants
- Fever >38.5°C lasting >48 hours or other concerns → call pediatrician

### Preterm/Low Birthweight Babies
- Vaccines are given based on chronological age (date of birth), NOT corrected gestational age
- Preterm babies are MORE vulnerable to vaccine-preventable diseases — do not delay
- Exception: for HepB at birth — if baby weighs <2 kg and mother is HepB-negative, physician may defer to 1 month

## Warning Signs 🚨
After vaccination, contact a doctor or emergency room if baby has:
- High-pitched inconsolable crying lasting >3 hours
- Fever >40°C (104°F)
- Seizures or convulsions
- Extreme drowsiness / difficult to wake
- Severe allergic reaction: hives, swelling of face/throat, difficulty breathing (within 15–30 min of vaccination — very rare)
