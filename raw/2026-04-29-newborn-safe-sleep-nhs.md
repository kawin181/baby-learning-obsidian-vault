# Newborn Safe Sleep — NHS

## Key Points
- Always place baby on **back** in the **feet-to-foot position** (feet touching the foot of the cot to prevent sliding under covers)
- Same room as parents for all sleep for the **first 6 months minimum**
- Keep cot clear: **no pillows, duvets, bumpers, soft toys, pods or nests**
- Baby sleeping bags reduce SIDS risk by preventing baby from sliding under bedding — ensure correct fit around shoulders
- Room temperature **16–20 °C**; use a room thermometer
- Remove hats immediately when coming indoors, even if it wakes baby
- **No smoking** during pregnancy or around baby; do not allow others to smoke in the same room
- **Dummies** at sleep time may lower SIDS risk
- **Breastfeeding** reduces SIDS risk
- Keep vaccinations up to date
- **Never sleep on sofa or armchair** with baby — highest risk position for SIDS

## Raw Content
### The Feet-to-Foot Principle
Place baby at the foot end of the cot so their feet touch the foot board. This prevents slipping beneath loose covers. Tuck in sheets and blankets firmly under the mattress.

### Temperature Guidance
A room thermometer is recommended. Overheating increases SIDS risk. Check if baby is too warm by feeling the back of the neck — should be warm, not hot or sweaty. Remove extra layers rather than adjust room temperature mid-night.

### Co-sleeping Risk Stratification
The NHS identifies the following as particularly high-risk co-sleeping situations:
- On a sofa or armchair (highest risk)
- When either parent smokes (even if not at time of sleep)
- When either parent has consumed alcohol
- When either parent has taken sedating medication
- When baby was premature (<37 weeks) or low birth weight (<2.5 kg)

### Dummies
The mechanism by which dummies reduce SIDS risk is not fully established, but evidence supports a protective effect. If breastfeeding, wait until breastfeeding is well established before introducing.
