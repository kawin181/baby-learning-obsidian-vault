# Vitamin D Supplementation in Newborns — NHS (UK)

## Key Points
- Breastfed babies: 8.5–10 micrograms (340–400 IU) vitamin D daily from birth
- Formula-fed babies: no supplement needed if drinking >500ml/day of fortified formula
- Supplement regardless of whether mother takes vitamin D herself
- Do NOT combine cod liver oil with vitamin drops (double-dosing risk)
- Free vitamin drops available via Healthy Start programme (UK)
- Only supplements containing vitamins A, C, and D are officially recommended

## Raw Content

### UK Dose
- **0–12 months breastfed:** 8.5–10 micrograms per day (equivalent ~340–400 IU)
- **1–4 years:** 10 micrograms per day (400 IU)
- Delivered as liquid drops

### Who Needs It
- **Breastfed babies:** From birth, regardless of mother's own vitamin D status
- **Partially breastfed:** Should also supplement
- **Formula-fed babies:** Not needed if taking >500ml (1 pint) per day of fortified formula

### How to Administer
- Drop onto a sterilized teaspoon and give by mouth, aimed at inside of cheek
- OR drop onto breast/nipple area before feeding
- OR give directly with a dropper while baby is upright

### Safety Note
- **Do NOT give cod liver oil + vitamin drops simultaneously** — cod liver oil also contains vitamins A and D, which can cause toxicity if combined
- Use only one appropriately-dosed supplement

### Sun Exposure
- Sunlight is the primary natural source of vitamin D, but infants should still supplement
- Outdoor time with sun protection still recommended — supplements ensure adequate intake year-round

### Access
- UK: Free vitamin drops via Healthy Start programme for qualifying families
- Health visitor can advise on suitable products
