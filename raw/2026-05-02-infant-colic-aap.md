# Infant Colic — AAP HealthyChildren

## Key Points
- About 1 in 5 babies develop colic, typically starting between weeks 2–4 of life
- Defined by inconsolable crying, often with leg-pulling, gas, and intensification in early evening
- Cause may be immature nervous system, food sensitivities, or unusual sensitivity to stimulation
- Resolves by 3–4 months; occasionally persists to 6 months
- Breastfeeding mothers may trial eliminating: milk, caffeine, onions, cabbage (one at a time, 2 weeks each)
- Formula-fed: discuss protein hydrolysate formula with pediatrician
- Comfort techniques: carrier walking, white noise rocking, pacifier, tummy-down on knees, swaddling, massage
- Parents must take breaks — even 1–2 hours away helps maintain positive attitude
- Never shake baby — warn parents of shaken baby syndrome risk

## Raw Content
**Prevalence:** ~20% of all infants develop colic.

**Onset:** Typically weeks 2–4; may persist until 3–6 months.

**Symptoms:** Inconsolable screaming; legs pulled up or extended; passing gas; worsening in early evening (cluster crying pattern).

**Possible causes (AAP):**
- Immature or hypersensitive nervous system
- Inability to self-soothe in early weeks
- Breastfeeding: sensitivity to maternal diet (dairy, caffeine, onions, cabbage)
- Formula: cow's milk protein sensitivity (rare, <5% of cases)
- Underlying conditions (e.g., hernia) — rare, must be ruled out

**Management:**
- Dietary modification (breastfeeding): eliminate suspected foods one at a time; 2 weeks per food
- Formula switch: protein hydrolysate formula if cow's milk protein suspected
- Soothing: baby carrier walk, rhythmic rocking + white noise, pacifier, tummy-down across parent's knees with back rub, swaddling, baby massage

**Parental wellbeing:**
- Parents should ask for relief from a partner, family member, or trusted friend
- 1–2 hours away from crying baby helps reset parental patience
- Screen for postpartum depression if parents are overwhelmed
- NEVER shake infant — shaken baby syndrome risk is highest during colic periods
