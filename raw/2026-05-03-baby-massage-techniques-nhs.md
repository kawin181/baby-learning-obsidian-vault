# Baby Massage Techniques — NHS Cambridge University Hospitals

## Key Points
- Gentle nurturing touch can begin at birth
- Strengthens the physical and emotional bond between infant and parent/carer
- Linked to improved sleep patterns for infant and parents
- Increases oxytocin ("happy hormones") in both baby and parent
- Evidence shows benefit for NICU preterm infants: shorter hospital stay, reduced pain, better weight gain
- Cochrane systematic review notes limited high-quality evidence — more research needed

## Raw Content

### Benefits for Infants
- **Interaction & Bonding:** Strengthens physical and emotional bond between infant and parent/primary carer
- **Physical Development:** Stimulates body systems; supports improved feeding, weight gain, sensory development
- **Colic Relief:** May help reduce discomfort from colic and constipation
- **Sleep:** Linked to improved sleep patterns; one study (120 mothers, babies 3–6 months) showed increased nightly sleep duration and fewer night wakings

### Benefits for Parents
- Deepened understanding of baby's needs and communication cues
- Stress reduction through increased oxytocin
- Enhanced parental confidence and wellbeing

### When to Start
- Gentle touch can begin at birth
- More structured massage may start from 32 weeks corrected gestation (for preterm infants)
- For full-term newborns: from birth, gentle; more intentional technique from ~2 weeks

### NHS Oil Guidance
- **Avoid oil in the first month** — allows skin time to develop its protective barrier layer
- After first month: unscented, mild baby-safe oils appropriate

### Evidence Base
- Maternal anxiety reduced when infant massage taught during hospital stay (improved attachment)
- Preterm NICU evidence: shorter length of stay, reduced pain scores, improved weight gain, better neurodevelopment outcomes
- Cochrane review caution: many studies are small/low-quality; reported benefits need larger trials

### Mechanism
- Promotes release of oxytocin in both parent and infant
- Allows one-to-one quality time supporting emotional regulation and trust
