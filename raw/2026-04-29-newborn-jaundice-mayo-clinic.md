# Newborn Jaundice — Mayo Clinic

## Key Points
- **Yellowing of skin and whites of eyes** is the main sign of infant jaundice — usually appears within **2–4 days** after birth
- Jaundice progresses **head to toe**: starts face → spreads to chest, abdomen, legs
- Mild jaundice usually resolves **on its own within 2–3 weeks** without treatment
- **More frequent feedings** (8–12 times/day) lower bilirubin by increasing stool output
- **Phototherapy** (special blue-spectrum lamp) is first-line treatment for significant jaundice
- **Exchange transfusion** is used in severe cases unresponsive to phototherapy
- **Preterm babies** are at higher risk for jaundice and complications

## Raw Content
### Symptoms
- Yellow tinge to skin — press gently on forehead/nose to check; if skin looks yellow, jaundice is likely
- Yellowing of the whites of the eyes (scleral icterus)
- In severe cases: yellow tinge to palms and soles

### Causes
**Physiologic (normal):** immature liver, high red blood cell turnover
**Breastfeeding jaundice:** inadequate feeding in first days → fewer stools → less bilirubin eliminated
**Breast milk jaundice:** substances in mature breast milk slow bilirubin metabolism; appears week 1–2, can persist months; benign
**Blood type incompatibility (ABO/Rh):** mother's antibodies attack baby's red cells → rapid bilirubin rise
**Other:** G6PD deficiency, infection, birth trauma (cephalohematoma), polycythemia

### Treatment
1. **Observation and feeding support** — mild jaundice: ensure adequate intake, monitor bilirubin
2. **Phototherapy** — blue-green light breaks down bilirubin; baby placed under lamp with eyes shielded; feeding continues during breaks
3. **Exchange transfusion** — small amounts of blood repeatedly withdrawn and replaced; reserved for severe/unresponsive cases
4. **IVIG** — for hemolytic jaundice specifically

### Home Monitoring
Bring baby back to doctor at 3–5 days of life for bilirubin check. At home, check jaundice in natural light. Watch for worsening yellowing, poor feeding, extreme sleepiness, high-pitched crying — these require urgent evaluation.
