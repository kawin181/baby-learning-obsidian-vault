# Newborn Safe Sleep — AAP / HealthyChildren.org

## Key Points
- Place baby on their **back** for every sleep — naps and nighttime
- Use a **firm, flat sleep surface** (firm mattress in a crib or bassinet that meets safety standards); inclined surfaces >10° are unsafe
- Keep baby in **same room** as parents for at least the first 6 months (ideally 1 year), but on a **separate sleep surface**
- Keep sleep area **bare**: no pillows, loose blankets, bumpers, soft toys, positioners, or wedges
- Breastfeeding **reduces SIDS risk** — any amount of breastfeeding is beneficial
- **Pacifier** at sleep time is protective against SIDS (introduce after breastfeeding is established)
- Avoid overheating — room temperature 16–20 °C; dress baby in one layer more than an adult
- **No smoke exposure** — prenatal or postnatal; smoking is one of the strongest SIDS risk factors
- **No bed-sharing** on sofas, armchairs, or with adults under the influence of alcohol/sedatives
- **Tummy time** when awake and supervised — start soon after birth, build to 15–30 min/day by 7 weeks; promotes motor development and helps prevent positional plagiocephaly
- Skin-to-skin care recommended immediately after birth; once done, place infant in supine position in bassinet
- Routine **immunizations** reduce SIDS risk

## Raw Content
The American Academy of Pediatrics (AAP) 2022 updated safe sleep guidelines represent the most comprehensive evidence-based framework for reducing sleep-related infant deaths, which account for ~3,500 deaths per year in the US.

### The ABCs of Safe Sleep
- **A**lone — baby sleeps alone on a separate surface
- **B**ack — always on back, never stomach or side
- **C**rib/Bassinet — firm, flat, safety-certified surface

### New in 2022 Guidelines
- Inclined sleep products (rockers, bouncers, car seats outside vehicles) explicitly deemed unsafe for unsupervised sleep
- Stronger language against bed-sharing devices (in-bed sleepers, nest products)
- Emphasis on eliminating soft bedding of any kind

### Pacifier Use
Use at sleep time beginning at birth if not breastfeeding, or once breastfeeding is established. Does not need to be reinserted after baby falls asleep.

### Room-Sharing vs Bed-Sharing
Room-sharing (baby in same room, different surface) reduces SIDS risk by up to 50%. Bed-sharing is not recommended; if a parent falls asleep while feeding, moving the baby to a safe surface as soon as waking is recommended.
