# Newborn Sleep Patterns & Schedules — NHS

## Key Points
- Total newborn sleep: 8–18 hours per day (wide normal range)
- 59% of parents report their baby (under 12 months) sleeps less than 4 hours continuously (Lullaby Trust survey)
- Babies will not sleep through the night early — small stomachs require 2–3 hourly feeds
- Simple bedtime routine from birth helps entrain sleep: bath → clean nappy → dim lights → story → cuddle
- Day/night differentiation: bright/active/noisy days vs quiet/dim nights
- Room-share for at least first 6 months
- Babies do not need complete silence — some background noise is fine
- By 3–6 months: some babies sleep 5–8 hours at night as feeds reduce

## Raw Content

### Sleep Duration
Newborn total daily sleep varies enormously — some sleep as little as 8 hours, others up to 18 hours; both are within the normal range. "Your baby will have their own pattern of waking and sleeping, and it's unlikely to be the same as other babies you know."

### Why Babies Wake at Night
Babies wake because they are hungry and have small stomachs. Night waking is normal and expected. According to a Lullaby Trust survey, 59% of parents with babies under 12 months report their infant sleeps less than 4 continuous hours.

### Establishing Routines Early
Simple bedtime routine from birth:
1. Bath (or gentle wash)
2. Fresh nappy
3. Dimmed lights
4. Quiet story or lullaby
5. Goodnight cuddle

Consistency matters more than the specific activities. The NHS notes that as the baby grows, keeping a similar bedtime routine helps signal sleep time.

### Day/Night Differentiation
- **Daytime**: Open curtains, keep normal household noise, engage when baby is awake
- **Night**: Keep lights low, speak quietly, avoid playing or stimulating the baby during night feeds and changes

### Noise Tolerance
"It's good to get your baby used to sleeping through a certain amount of noise." Parents do not need to maintain complete silence — moderate background noise is fine and helpful.

### Feeding Partner Support
If breastfeeding, once a routine is established, partners can occasionally give expressed breast milk at night so the feeding parent can rest.

### Sleep Milestones
- 0–6 weeks: Feeds every 2–3 hours; no sustained sleep; wakes frequently at night
- 3–6 months: May extend to 5–8 hours at night; fewer night feeds required

### Room-Sharing
For at least the first 6 months, the baby should sleep in the same room as the parent(s), both day and night. This is associated with a lower risk of SIDS.
