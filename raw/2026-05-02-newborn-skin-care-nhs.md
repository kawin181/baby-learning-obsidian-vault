# Newborn Skin Care & Baby Bath — NHS

## Key Points
- Daily bathing not necessary — "top and tail" (face, neck, hands, bottom) is sufficient for daily hygiene
- Top and tail technique: separate cotton wool pieces per eye; wipe nose outward; clean around (not inside) ears; warm water and cotton wool for bottom
- Umbilical cord: clean only if soiled with pee/poo; cotton wool + warm water; pat dry
- Never leave baby unattended in bath — babies can drown in centimeters of water
- Older children should not supervise babies in baths
- Water: warm to wrist/elbow; head must remain clear of water
- Bath seats not recommended by RoSPA — collapse risk and drowning risk
- Talk to baby during wash — helps them relax
- Products: plain water or baby-safe liquid cleanser; check any oil/lotion is baby-formulated
- Nail care: baby nail clippers or safety scissors; best done when baby is asleep

## Raw Content
**Top and tail washing (daily hygiene without full bath):**
- Keeps baby clean between baths
- Baby on knee or changing mat, wrapped except for wash area
- Eyes: damp cotton wool, separate piece each eye, wipe from inner to outer corner
- Ears: clean around outer ear only; never insert anything inside
- Face, neck, hands: warm water and cotton wool
- Genitals/bottom: warm water and cotton wool; girls: wipe front to back
- Umbilical cord: only if soiled; clean gently; pat dry thoroughly to prevent infection

**Full bath frequency:**
- Not required daily; bathing too often strips natural skin oils
- Top and tail most days; full bath every few days is sufficient

**Full bath safety (NHS / RoSPA):**
- Run bath; test temperature with wrist or elbow
- NEVER leave baby unattended, even to answer phone
- Older siblings must not watch baby alone during bath
- Babies can drown in only a few centimeters of water
- Bath seats: not recommended — can tip over; do not provide safety

**Talking during bath:**
- Talking/singing during washing helps baby relax
- Builds parent-baby bond

**Products:**
- Plain warm water preferred for newborns
- If using a cleanser: liquid, fragrance-free, baby-formulated
- Avoid adult soaps, bubble baths, and harsh products

**Nail care:**
- Use baby nail clippers or safety scissors (not adult tools)
- Best done while baby sleeps — hands stay still
