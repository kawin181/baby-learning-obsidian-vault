# Pacifier Use & Safety in Newborns — AAP / HealthyChildren.org

## Key Points
- Wait 3–4 weeks for breastfeeding to be established before introducing a pacifier
- Offering a pacifier at nap time and bedtime reduces the risk of SIDS
- The SIDS-protective effect persists even if the pacifier falls out during sleep — no need to reinsert
- Never tie a pacifier to the crib, neck, or hand — strangulation/injury risk
- The shield must be ≥1.5 inches (3.8 cm) across with ventilation holes
- Choose one-piece molded pacifiers to prevent choking from separated parts
- Boil or dishwash before first use; clean frequently until baby is 6 months old
- Inspect regularly for discoloured or torn rubber; replace if damaged
- Do not use pacifiers to replace or delay feedings
- Extended use past 2–4 years may affect dental alignment and mouth shape

## Raw Content

### When to Introduce
If breastfeeding, wait until nursing is well established — approximately 3 to 4 weeks. If formula feeding, a pacifier can be introduced at any time from birth.

### SIDS Risk Reduction
Pacifier use at nap and bedtime is one of the most evidence-backed ways to reduce the risk of SIDS. The protective mechanism is not fully understood but the association is strong. Notably, the protective effect holds even if the pacifier falls out of the baby's mouth after sleep onset — parents do not need to reinsert it.

### Physical Safety Requirements
- Shield diameter: at least 1.5 inches (3.8 cm)
- Shield material: firm plastic with ventilation holes
- Construction: ideally one-piece moulded plastic — no parts that can detach
- Avoid using bottle nipples with tops taped together as improvised pacifiers — suction can dislodge the nipple and cause choking

### Strangulation Hazard
Never attach a pacifier to the crib, and never tie one around the baby's neck or hand. Never use a pacifier attached to clothing clips or stuffed toys — all are strangulation or choking risks.

### Cleaning
- Sterilise (boil or dishwasher) before first use
- Clean frequently until baby is 6 months old to minimise germ exposure
- Do not "clean" by sucking the pacifier yourself — transfers adult oral bacteria to infant

### Maintenance
Check periodically for discolouration or tearing of the rubber/silicone nipple. Replace when damaged or per manufacturer's expiry guidance. Note: adult-sized pacifier users risk swallowing smaller newborn-sized ones — size matters.

### Feeding Interaction
Do not offer pacifier when baby is hungry. Only use it when you are certain the baby has been fed and is using the pacifier purely for comfort sucking (non-nutritive sucking).

### Long-Term Dental Consideration
Extended strong sucking use beyond 2–4 years of age may cause changes in mouth shape and tooth alignment. Most children stop naturally before school age due to peer pressure. If dental changes are noted, consult a paediatrician or paediatric dentist.
