# Cradle Cap (Seborrheic Dermatitis)

## Summary
Cradle cap is an extremely common, harmless scalp condition affecting approximately 70% of babies by 3 months of age. It appears as greasy, yellowish, scaly patches on the scalp and sometimes on the face, neck, and diaper area. It is not itchy, not painful, not contagious, and not caused by poor hygiene — and it almost always resolves on its own within weeks to months.

## What Research Says
- **Prevalence:** About 70% of 3-month-old babies have some degree of cradle cap (AAP).
- **Onset:** Typically appears between 3 weeks and 2 months after birth.
- **Cause:** Unknown. Leading theories include: (1) reaction to naturally occurring skin yeast (*Malassezia*), or (2) maternal hormones causing excess sebaceous gland activity after birth (AAP).
- **Self-limiting:** Resolves on its own in most cases by 12 months; no treatment is medically required (AAP, NHS).
- **Oil warning:** NHS current guidance explicitly warns against olive oil and peanut oil — olive oil has been shown to damage the infant skin barrier despite being a long-standing traditional remedy. Coconut oil is safe.
- **Beyond the scalp:** Can appear in skin folds (armpits, neck, groin) and diaper area — this is normal and still benign.

## Practical Guidelines

### First-line Home Care
1. **Wash every other day** with mild, unscented baby shampoo — regular washing prevents scale build-up
2. **Gently loosen scales** with a soft brush or fine-tooth comb during or after shampooing
3. **For stubborn thick scales:** Apply coconut oil, mineral oil, or petroleum jelly to the scalp and leave overnight. Wash off gently in the morning with baby shampoo and soft brush
4. **Diaper area involvement:** Apply barrier cream/emollient to affected skin folds

### Safe Products
| Recommended | Avoid |
|-------------|-------|
| Coconut oil | Olive oil (damages skin barrier) |
| Mineral oil | Peanut/nut-based oils (allergy risk) |
| Petroleum jelly (Vaseline) | Adult shampoos |
| Mild baby shampoo | Fragrant/scented products |
| Emollient moisturizer | Medicated adult products |

### Important: Do Not Pick
- Never scratch or pick at scales — open skin can become infected with bacteria
- Let the shampoo and oil do the work over time

### Timeline
- Most cases clear within a few weeks to months of gentle home care
- Persistent cases can last until 12 months
- Occasionally lingers into toddlerhood — still benign

## Thai Context
Traditional Thai advice often includes applying สะระแหน่ (peppermint oil) or น้ำมันมะกอก (olive oil) to scalp conditions. Current NHS evidence suggests olive oil should be avoided as it damages the skin barrier. น้ำมันมะพร้าว (coconut oil) is the preferred alternative — it is culturally familiar, widely available in Thailand, and clinically safer for infant skin.

## Warning Signs 🚨
See a doctor if cradle cap shows any of the following:
- Thick, crusted, weepy, or bleeding scales
- Pus or foul odor (signs of bacterial infection)
- Visible swelling or warmth around affected area
- Appears to cause pain or significant irritation to baby
- Spreads rapidly or covers the whole body
- Does not improve at all after 4+ weeks of home treatment
- Rash in skin folds that looks bright red and inflamed (may be a different condition)
