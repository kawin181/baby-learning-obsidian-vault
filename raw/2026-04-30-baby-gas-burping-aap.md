# Baby Gas & Burping Techniques — AAP / HealthyChildren.org

## Key Points
- Burp baby during AND after every feeding, not just at the end
- Most effective burping position: upright with head on parent's shoulder, gently pat back
- Bicycle-leg exercise while baby lies on back helps move trapped gas
- Slow bottle flow reduces air swallowed during feeds
- Vented, angled, or collapsible bottles are designed to reduce air intake
- Simethicone (gas drops): no definitive clinical evidence of benefit; AAP notes pediatricians increasingly advise against use
- Gassiness is normal in newborns — digestive system is still maturing

## Raw Content
### Burping Positions
1. **Over the shoulder** — hold baby upright, head resting on your shoulder; support head and back; gently pat or rub back
2. **Sitting upright on lap** — hold baby seated on your lap, supporting chin/chest with one hand; pat back with other hand
3. **Face-down across lap** — lay baby face-down across your knees; support head above body level; gently pat back

### When to Burp
- During feeding: every 2–3 oz for bottle-fed babies; switch sides for breastfed babies
- After each feeding session
- If baby seems uncomfortable or pulls away from breast/bottle

### Gas Relief Beyond Burping
- **Bicycle legs**: lay baby supine (face-up), gently move legs in cycling motion — mechanical action massages intestines and helps move trapped gas
- **Tummy massage**: gentle circular massage of abdomen in clockwise direction
- **Upright time**: keep baby upright 20–30 minutes after feeds

### Feeding Technique to Reduce Gas
- Keep bottle at ~45° angle so nipple is always filled with milk (prevents air gulping)
- Choose slow-flow nipples for bottle-fed newborns
- For breastfed babies: ensure good latch (Latch) to prevent air ingestion

### Gas Drops
- Simethicone available OTC — supposed to break up gas bubbles
- AAP: studies show simethicone does NOT help with colic; no definitive evidence of benefit
- Increasing number of pediatricians recommend avoiding them
