# Baby Massage Techniques — AAP HealthyChildren.org

## Key Points
- Infant massage uses deep-touch pressure shown to have a calming, organizing effect on the nervous system
- Recommended to start 1–2 weeks after birth once umbilical cord has healed
- Use at least 30 minutes after feeding to prevent discomfort or spitting up
- Incorporates daily into routines: post-bath, diaper changes, feeding times
- Safe oils: natural coconut oil; avoid adult massage oils, hand lotions, nut-based oils

## Raw Content

### Benefits for Baby
- Eases into healthy, restful sleep
- Lowers stress hormones, promotes calm
- Improves muscle relaxation and flexibility
- Better digestion — reduces constipation, gas, colic symptoms
- Enhanced circulation and immune function
- Supports sensory development

### Benefits for Parents
- Builds confidence in daily caregiving
- Sharpens ability to read baby's cues and preferences
- Deepens parent-infant bonding
- Stress relief for the caregiver

### Six Core Techniques (AAP "CALM" approach)
1. **Back** — Apply deep pressure from shoulders to lower back; good during tummy time to improve comfort
2. **Hands** — Firmly hold palms; use circular motions to ease tight fists
3. **Feet** — Hold ankle, stroke foot firmly; gently tug each toe with deep pressure (no light tickling — startles baby)
4. **Shoulders** — Rub shoulders and arms with firm downward strokes while baby sits supported
5. **Full Body** — Move firm pressure down entire body along the sides or center
6. **Head** — Stroke backward over crown; trace jawline down to chin; watch for comfort cues

### Timing Rules
- Minimum 30–45 minutes after feeding
- Start with 5–10 minute sessions; gradually increase
- Offer daily — ideally same time each day

### Oil Safety
- **Recommended:** Natural coconut oil
- **Avoid:** Adult massage oils, hand lotions, nut-based oils (allergy risk)
- Non-medicated baby rubs: safe at 3+ months only

### Key Mechanism
- Deep-touch pressure activates the parasympathetic nervous system within moments
- Babies' autonomic nervous systems respond to firm, gentle pressure — light tickling is counterproductive
