# Newborn Fever — AAP / HealthyChildren.org

## Key Points
- Fever is defined as a rectal temperature ≥ 100.4°F (38°C)
- Any fever in infants 3 months or younger requires immediate pediatric evaluation, even without other symptoms
- Rectal thermometer is the most accurate method for infants under 3 months
- Ear (tympanic) thermometers are unreliable in babies under 6 months due to small ear canals
- Temperature-sensitive forehead strips are not reliable
- AAP 2021 guideline stratifies febrile infants 8–60 days into three groups: 8–21 days, 22–28 days, 29–60 days
- ~14 per 1,000 healthy full-term infants develop fever between 8–60 days old
- >10% of febrile infants are diagnosed with a urinary tract infection (UTI)
- <0.05% of febrile infants develop meningitis (the most serious infection)
- Other serious infections: sepsis, pneumonia, bacteremia, gastroenteritis

## Raw Content
Fever in a newborn or young infant (0–3 months) is a medical emergency situation requiring prompt professional evaluation. Unlike older children, infants this young cannot localize infection well and fever may be the only outward sign of meningitis, sepsis, or bacteremia.

### Temperature Definition
- Fever: rectal temp ≥ 38°C (100.4°F)
- Rectal measurement is gold standard for this age group

### AAP 2021 Guideline (infants 8–60 days)
The guideline provides evidence-based risk stratification:
- Low-risk criteria involve biomarkers: procalcitonin, absolute neutrophil count (ANC), urinalysis
- Low-risk infants 29–60 days may be managed without lumbar puncture if certain criteria met
- High-risk or younger infants (8–28 days) typically require full sepsis workup and hospitalization

### When to Call Immediately
- Baby ≤ 3 months with any fever (≥ 38°C rectally)
- Difficulty breathing
- Febrile seizure lasting > 15 minutes → call emergency services (911)
- Suspected heatstroke

### How to Take Temperature Correctly
1. Use digital rectal thermometer with petroleum jelly
2. Lay baby on back, flex knees to chest
3. Insert thermometer 1/2–1 inch into rectum
4. Hold still for ~2 minutes or until beep
5. Read and record result
