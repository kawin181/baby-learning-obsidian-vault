# Newborn Vision Development — AAP (HealthyChildren.org)

## Key Points
- Newborns can see light, shapes, and faces, and detect movement at birth
- Optimal viewing distance at birth: 20–30 cm (8–12 inches) — exactly the distance during feeding
- Random eye wandering/crossing is normal in newborns; should resolve by 2–3 months
- By 3 months: can focus on faces, follow moving objects with eyes, eye coordination improves
- By 4 months: reaches for objects, color vision improves, interest in circular patterns (bullseyes, spirals, faces)
- By 6 months: visual discrimination — can distinguish between different objects
- Vision problems may become irreversible if not detected early — regular well-child eye checks essential

## Raw Content

### Birth to Early Weeks
- Newborn visual capability: perceives light, basic shapes, and faces; detects movement
- Distance: everything beyond 20–30 cm is blurry
- 20–30 cm is the typical distance between mother's face and baby during breastfeeding — evolutionary design
- Eye coordination: random wandering, crossing, unfocused movement — all normal
- Babies are drawn to high-contrast patterns and human faces

### Color Vision
- Newborns initially see in limited color — not full grayscale but limited color range
- Color perception improves significantly by 4 months
- Best stimulation: high-contrast black-and-white patterns, then introduce bold primary colors

### By 3 Months
- Able to focus on faces and close objects
- Can track/follow a moving object with eyes (smooth pursuit develops)
- Random eye wandering resolves — if not, flag to pediatrician
- Recognizes caregiver's face
- Begins to show interest in brightly colored objects

### By 4 Months
- Uses vision to detect objects, then reaches to grasp
- Color perception greatly improved — sees full color range
- Fascination with circular patterns (bull's-eyes, spirals) — mirrors facial feature interest
- Depth perception beginning to develop (binocular vision establishing)

### Stimulation Tips
- Hold baby 20–30 cm from your face and make eye contact
- Use black-and-white high-contrast books/cards in first 2 months
- Introduce bold-colored mobiles and toys after 3 months
- Change baby's position and visual environment regularly

### Warning Signs (from AAP)
- Can't make steady eye contact by 3 months
- Eyes still randomly wandering past 3 months
- Seems unable to see or track movement
- Persistent squinting beyond 4 months
- White pupil (leukocoria) — immediate referral needed
