# Formula Feeding Basics

## Summary
Iron-fortified infant formula is the safe, nutritionally complete alternative to breast milk for babies 0–12 months. The AAP recommends feeding 1–2 oz every 2–3 hours in the first week, gradually increasing to 3–4 oz every 3–4 hours by the end of month 1. Safe preparation — using the exact water-to-powder ratio, correct water temperature, and proper storage — is essential to prevent nutrient deficiency and bacterial illness.

## What Research Says
**Feeding amounts (0–3 months):**
- Week 1: 1–2 oz (30–60 ml) per feeding, every 2–3 hours
- By 1 month: 3–4 oz (90–120 ml) per feeding, every 3–4 hours, ~32 oz total per day
- General formula: 2.5 oz of formula per pound of body weight per day
- Maximum: 32 oz (960 ml) per 24 hours (AAP)
- Between 2–4 months, most formula-fed babies (>12 lbs) can drop nighttime feedings

**Why preparation accuracy matters:**
- Adding extra water (diluting) reduces caloric and nutrient density, slowing growth
- Over-dilution can cause hyponatremia (low blood sodium) leading to seizures — a documented pediatric emergency
- Under-measuring water (over-concentrating) stresses kidneys and causes dehydration

**Cronobacter safety (CDC):**
- Powdered formula is not sterile; it can contain Cronobacter sakazakii
- Prepare powdered formula with water heated to ≥70°C (158°F), then cool before feeding
- Premixed liquid formula does not carry this risk

**Storage evidence:**
- Prepared formula not yet fed: safe 2 hours at room temperature, 24 hours refrigerated
- Once feeding has begun: discard within 1 hour (saliva introduces bacteria rapidly)

## Practical Guidelines
1. **Measure water first** into the bottle, then add the exact number of level scoops of formula — never reverse this order
2. **Use safe water** — boil tap water if unsure; cool to room temperature before measuring
3. **For powdered formula**: mix with water at ~70°C, shake well, then cool under running cold water before feeding
4. **Warming**: formula does not need to be warm — if preferred, warm by setting bottle in warm water; NEVER microwave
5. **Test temperature** on inside of wrist before feeding
6. **Watch hunger cues**: rooting, lip-smacking, sucking on hands
7. **Watch fullness cues**: turning head away, becoming distracted, slowing sucking
8. **Pace the feeding**: hold bottle horizontal, let baby control the suck rate — prevents overfeeding
9. **Burp** midway through and after feeding to release swallowed air
10. **Store opened powder** in original container with tight lid, in a cool dry place — use within 1 month

## Thai Context
- นมผสม (infant formula) is widely available in Thailand; choose iron-fortified brands approved by อย. (FDA Thailand)
- Boiling water is standard in Thai households — use it for formula preparation, but cool to ~70°C before adding powder (not boiling hot)
- Avoid "follow-on" formulas (นมช่วงที่ 2) for babies under 6 months; these are not appropriate substitutes for stage 1 formula
- The Thai Ministry of Public Health promotes breast milk as primary; formula use is supported when breastfeeding is not possible or insufficient

## Warning Signs 🚨
- Baby not gaining weight adequately (see [[wiki/newborn-weight-gain]])
- Fewer than 6 wet diapers per day after day 5
- Baby consistently refusing formula or vomiting large amounts after every feeding
- Signs of allergic reaction: rash, hives, difficulty breathing, excessive fussiness after feeding
- Stool that is white/pale, bloody, or black (after the first few days)
- Baby seems dehydrated: dry mouth, no tears, sunken fontanelle (กระหม่อม)
