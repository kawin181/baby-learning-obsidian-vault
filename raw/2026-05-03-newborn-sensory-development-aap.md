# Newborn Sensory Development — AAP HealthyChildren

## Key Points
- Newborns see best at 8–12 inches (20–30 cm) — roughly the distance during feeding
- Eyes may occasionally cross or wander in the first 2–3 months; this is normal
- By 3 months, babies can focus on faces and track moving objects
- Newborns prefer pleasant smells (milk, vanilla, banana, sugar) and turn away from alcohol or vinegar
- By end of the first week, breastfed babies recognize and turn toward their own mother's breast pad
- Babies respond to soft textures (flannel, satin) positively and to rough materials negatively
- Gentle stroking calms babies; rough handling triggers crying
- At 2 months: watches faces intently, eyes follow moving objects, reacts to loud noises, smiles back
- At 4 months: sees several yards, responds to full color spectrum, may prefer circular patterns

## Raw Content

### Vision
Newborns can perceive light, shapes, and faces and detect movement. Best visual focus is at 8–12 cm — the distance from baby's eyes to a parent's face during nursing.

Eye crossing or wandering is normal in the first 2–3 months as the baby learns to coordinate both eyes. By 3 months, babies should be able to:
- Focus on nearby objects and faces
- Follow a moving object with their eyes
- Start using hands and eyes in coordination

### Smell & Taste
Newborns breathe deeply to detect pleasant smells and turn away from unpleasant ones. By the end of the first week, a breastfeeding baby can already identify their mother's milk by smell. Breast milk's natural sweetness aligns with babies' innate preference for sweet tastes.

### Touch
Infants respond positively to soft textures and gentle handling. Firm but gentle stroking promotes relaxation. Quality of touch communicates emotion — rough handling causes distress, gentle holding promotes calm. Soothing techniques include holding, stroking, rocking, and cuddling.

### Hearing
Babies naturally prefer the human voice. From birth they are learning about speech and language just by listening. They recognize familiar voices early and by 2 months react to loud noises.
