# Baby Growth Spurts & Cluster Feeding — AAP (HealthyChildren.org)

## Key Points
- Average monthly weight gain 1–3 months: 0.7–0.9 kg (1.5–2 lbs) per month
- Average monthly length gain: 2.5–4 cm (1–1.5 inches) per month
- Head circumference growth: ~1.25 cm (0.5 inch) per month
- At 2 months baby appears round and chubby; posterior fontanel closes ~3 months
- Growth spurts trigger increased feeding demand — baby may feed every 1 hour during spurts
- Normal newborn feeding: 8–12 times per day (breastfed); formula-fed on demand
- Parents should "be prepared for growth spurts that will inevitably result in your baby's increased demand for more milk"

## Raw Content

### Growth Rates (1–3 Months)
- Weight: 0.7–0.9 kg/month
- Length: 2.5–4 cm/month
- Head circumference: ~1.25 cm/month
- Head grows disproportionately fast initially; body catches up later
- By 3 months: posterior soft spot (fontanel) typically closes

### Growth Spurts
- Occur as intermittent small or large spurts
- Vary by age, sex, maturational status, and season
- Timing in first 3 months: commonly around 2 weeks, 6 weeks, and 3 months
- Trigger increased feeding demand — breastfed baby may demand feeds every 30–60 minutes
- Last typically 2–7 days before settling back to routine

### Feeding During Growth Spurts
- Breastfed babies: follow baby's cues; do not supplement formula unless medically indicated
- Supply-demand mechanism: more frequent nursing signals the body to produce more milk
- Parents often mistake spurt feeding for "not enough milk" — this is the most common misinterpretation
- Formula-fed babies may need slightly more per feed during spurts

### When to Call the Doctor
- Baby not regaining birth weight by 2 weeks
- Weight loss >10% of birth weight
- Fewer than 6 wet diapers/day after Day 4
- No weight gain over 2 weeks at any point
