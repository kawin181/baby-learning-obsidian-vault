# Kangaroo Care & Skin-to-Skin Contact — NHS Maidstone and Tunbridge Wells

## Key Points
- Kangaroo care = skin-to-skin contact; baby placed naked against parent's bare chest immediately after birth
- Works after all delivery types: vaginal, forceps, vacuum, or caesarean
- Baby covered with warm towel/blanket and knitted hat to minimize heat loss
- Recommended "as much as possible" during days and weeks following birth
- NHS trust research on kangaroo care won two national awards (2011–2012)

## Raw Content

### Benefits for Baby
- Regulates body temperature and blood sugar levels
- Calms breathing and heart rate during birth recovery
- Reduces stress and separation anxiety → supports brain development
- Promotes growth and weight gain
- Initiates breastfeeding instincts (baby instinctively seeks breast when skin-to-skin)
- Transfers maternal skin bacteria to infant — protective microbiome seeding

### Benefits for Parents
- Stimulates milk production in nursing mothers
- Facilitates bonding with newborn
- Encourages successful breastfeeding initiation
- Reduces stress and anxiety in parents
- Supports recovery from postpartum depression and low mood

### How to Practice (NHS Protocol)
- After delivery, staff dry the baby and place naked on parent's bare chest
- Baby covered with warm towel or blanket
- Knitted hat placed on baby to minimize heat loss through head
- Parents encouraged to continue in days/weeks following birth
- KangaWraps can help keep hands free during contact

### NHS Evidence & Awards
- NHS Trust research won 2 national awards (2011, 2012)
- Finalist at Royal College of Midwives awards (2015)
- Additional NHS sources confirm: improved autonomic stability, neurobehavioral development, non-pharmacological pain relief
