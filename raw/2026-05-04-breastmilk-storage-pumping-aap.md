# Breastmilk Storage & Pumping — AAP / HealthyChildren.org

## Key Points
- Room temperature (up to 25°C/77°F): up to 4 hours; chill as soon as possible
- Refrigerator (≤4°C/39°F): best within 4 days; store at back, not door
- Standard freezer (0°F/−18°C): up to 9 months
- Deep freezer (−20°C/−4°F): up to 12 months
- Thawed milk: best within 24 hours; never refreeze
- Leftover milk after baby drinks: use within 2 hours, then discard
- NEVER microwave breastmilk — creates hot spots that burn baby's mouth
- Store in 2–4 oz (60–120ml) portions to minimise waste
- Milk expands when frozen — do not fill container to the top
- Label each container with expression date and baby's name

## Raw Content

### Storage Time Table

| Location | Maximum Time | Notes |
|----------|-------------|-------|
| Room temperature (≤25°C) | 4 hours | Chill asap; up to 6–8 hours if very cleanly expressed |
| Refrigerator (≤4°C) | 4 days (best) | Back of fridge, not door |
| Standard freezer (−18°C) | 9 months | |
| Deep freezer (−20°C) | 12 months | Colder = longer |

### Thawing
1. Overnight in refrigerator (safest)
2. Hold under warm running water
3. Set container in bowl of warm water

Do NOT thaw in microwave. Thawed milk stored in fridge: best within 24 hours (some sources allow 48–72 hours).

### Warming
- Set bottle/bag in bowl of warm water, or run under warm water
- Microwave is NOT safe — creates uneven hot spots
- Test temperature on inside of wrist before feeding

### Leftover Milk After Feeding
Use within 2 hours. If refrigerated immediately after partial feeding, can use for the next feed. Do not return leftover to the freezer.

### Container and Hygiene
- Wash hands before pumping or expressing
- Use sterilised glass or hard plastic containers, or special breast milk storage bags
- Portion size: 2–4 oz (60–120 ml) to avoid waste
- Do not fill containers completely (milk expands on freezing)
- Label with date, time, and baby's name (especially for childcare settings)
- Store at the back of the refrigerator or freezer (avoids temperature fluctuations)

### Pumping Notes
- Electric pumps are faster; manual pumps are more affordable
- Ensure pump flange/funnel fits correctly to avoid nipple trauma
- Clean all pump parts that contact milk after each use per manufacturer instructions
