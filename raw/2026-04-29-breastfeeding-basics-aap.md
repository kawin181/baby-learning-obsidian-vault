# Breastfeeding Basics — AAP / HealthyChildren.org

## Key Points
- Feed **8–12 times per 24 hours** (roughly every 2–3 hours); do not let newborn go >3–4 hours without feeding in first weeks
- A good **Latch** means: baby's mouth is wide open, lips flanged outward (like a fish), chin and nose close to breast, baby takes in not just nipple but significant portion of areola
- **Alignment**: ear-shoulder-hip of baby in a straight line; baby faces breast, not ceiling
- **Hunger cues** (feed before crying): rooting, sucking motions, hands to mouth — crying is a late cue
- **Breastfeeding reduces SIDS risk** — the AAP 2022 policy statement recommends exclusive breastfeeding for 6 months
- Monitor output: 6+ wet diapers and 3+ stools per day by day 4–5 confirms adequate intake
- Weight gain: lose up to 7–10% in first days, regain birth weight by 10–14 days
- Have latch assessed by a healthcare provider before discharge and again at 2–3 days post-discharge

## Raw Content
### Latch Technique (Step by Step)
1. Position yourself comfortably with good back support
2. Hold baby close — tummy-to-tummy contact
3. Support baby's head and neck; do not push the back of baby's head
4. Line up baby's nose to your nipple (not mouth to nipple) — this encourages wide gape
5. Wait for wide open mouth (yawn-like gape), then bring baby to breast quickly
6. Check: baby's chin touches breast first, lower lip is well below the nipple, nose is near but not buried in breast

### Common Holds
- **Cross-cradle**: good for newborns; provides head control
- **Cradle hold**: most common; comfortable once latch is established
- **Football/clutch hold**: useful after C-section or for large breasts
- **Laid-back / biological nurturing**: gravity helps baby achieve deep latch; good for early days

### Signs of Effective Feeding
- Rhythmic sucking and swallowing sounds
- Baby releases breast spontaneously when satisfied
- Breast softens after feeding
- Nipple looks round (not flattened/pinched) after removal

### Pain and Latch Problems
Some initial discomfort is normal. Sustained pain, blistered or cracked nipples, or a "lipstick-shaped" nipple after feeding all indicate a shallow latch. Seek lactation consultant support promptly.

### AAP 2022 Policy: Breastfeeding Recommendation
Exclusive breastfeeding for 6 months, then continued breastfeeding alongside complementary foods for 2 years or beyond. Any amount of breastfeeding confers health benefits.
