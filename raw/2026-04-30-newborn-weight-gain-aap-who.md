# Newborn Weight Gain & Growth Monitoring — AAP / WHO Growth Standards

## Key Points
- AAP and CDC recommend WHO growth charts for all term infants during the first 2 years
- WHO growth standards are based on data from 6 countries and represent optimal growth under ideal conditions
- Z scores (standard deviations from the mean) are the most accurate way to assess nutritional status
- Use 2.3rd and 97.7th percentiles to flag potentially suboptimal growth
- Typical weight gain rate: ~150–210 g per week (5–7 oz/week) in the first 3 months
- AAP weight-change nomogram: 50% of newborns are at or above birth weight by days 9–10
- 14% of vaginally delivered infants are NOT back to birth weight by day 14
- 24% of cesarean-delivered infants are NOT back to birth weight by day 14
- WHO Multicentre Growth Reference Study provides velocity charts for weight, length, and head circumference
- Z scores below –2 or above +2 warrant clinical evaluation

## Raw Content
The American Academy of Pediatrics (AAP) recommends using WHO Multicentre Growth Reference Study (MGRS) growth standards for monitoring term infants from birth through 24 months. These charts reflect growth under optimal conditions (exclusive breastfeeding, non-smoking environment, adequate nutrition, no chronic disease).

**Key assessment tools:**
- WHO weight-for-age charts (birth to 24 months)
- WHO length-for-age charts
- WHO head circumference-for-age charts
- Incremental growth velocity charts (weight gain per 2-week interval)

**Weight gain expectations (0–3 months):**
- Average: ~150–210 g/week (approximately 1 oz per day)
- By end of month 3, most babies have doubled their birth weight

**AAP Weight-Change Nomogram data (Flaherman et al., Pediatrics 2016):**
- Dataset: >100,000 births across U.S. hospitals
- 50th percentile: back to birth weight by day 9–10
- Maximum weight loss typically occurs at day 3 for vaginally delivered infants
- Babies losing >10% of birth weight should be evaluated for feeding adequacy

**Warning thresholds:**
- Weight loss >10% of birth weight → evaluate feeding
- Not back to birth weight by day 14 → clinical review
- Drop of 2 or more major percentile lines → investigate
